# Contributing to Quorum

Quorum is open source under the MIT license. Contributions are welcome when they sharpen the tool without breaking what it is.

## What fits

- Better interview questions, panel structures, or prioritization logic.
- Stronger guardrails for the operating contract.
- New illustrative examples (fictional composites only, no real client data).
- Clearer docs.

## What does not fit

- Anything that turns Quorum into a generic "ask AI for advice" tool. The gated roster and structured output are the point.
- Anything that weakens the Faithful Representation Gate.
- Anything that fabricates experts, quotes, metrics, or endorsements.
- Anything that publishes, sends, or ships on the user's behalf.

## How to contribute

1. Fork the repo.
2. Make the change on a branch.
3. Test it on a real run if you can.
4. Open a pull request that states what you changed, why, and how you tested it.

## Ground rules

- Keep the writing direct and free of em dashes, to match the house style.
- Do not add a dependency the skill can run without.
- If you propose a new default (a framework, a panel), explain the reasoning, not just the change.

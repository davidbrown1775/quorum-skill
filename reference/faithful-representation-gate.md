# The Faithful Representation Gate

A seat on the board is earned by a public body of work, not a familiar name. Quorum seats a person only when enough authored, public material exists to represent how they actually think. The goal is a faithful lens, not a caricature.

This gate applies to everyone: the experts Quorum proposes and the names the user brings.

## The bar

Seat a person only if they clear all of the following.

1. **A real body of work in their own words.** Multiple books, a sustained blog or newsletter, a deep catalog of articles, talks, or podcasts, or an active site Quorum can draw from. Sustained output over a meaningful period, not a single moment.
2. **Enough to ground the lens.** There is enough material to represent their actual frameworks, positions, and language, so the review reflects how they think rather than a guess.
3. **Public and attributable.** The material is public and clearly theirs.

## What fails the gate

- Thin footprints. One talk, one article, or a handful of posts is not enough.
- Someone who only "seems close" to the right lane without the corpus to back it.
- A private operator with no public body of work, however skilled.

## Living versus historical

- **Living or modern figures** qualify on an active site, a steady stream of articles or posts, interviews, or a published book.
- **Historical or deceased figures** qualify on multiple published books, or a large body of secondary work written about them and their methods.

## When the user proposes someone who fails

Do not seat them. Tell the user plainly why the person does not clear the bar, and offer a well-documented alternative in the same lane. The user can insist, but Quorum records the lower confidence and notes that the lens may be weak.

## Representation confidence

Label every seated expert:

- **High.** A large, direct corpus in their own words. The lens is well grounded.
- **Medium.** A real but smaller corpus, or heavier reliance on secondary sources. Usable, with some interpretation.
- **Low.** Just clears the bar, or was seated at the user's insistence. Treat findings from this lens with extra caution.

Surface the confidence next to each seat so the user sees how grounded each voice is.

## Represent, do not impersonate

Clearing the gate earns a lens, not a voice to fake. Apply the expert's frameworks. Never fabricate quotes or endorsements. See `operating-contract.md`.

# Example: two-person B2B SaaS

> Illustrative. Fictional composite. No real client data. Bracketed numbers are placeholders.

## Context
A two-person SaaS turns regulatory updates into plain-language summaries for compliance teams. It is live with [[X]] paying customers and a first sale closed. There is no written roadmap and the backlog lives in someone's head.

## Interview highlights
- Goal: reach [[$Y]] MRR in six months.
- Constraint: two people, building nights and weekends.
- Definition of good: a predictable pipeline they do not have to chase.

## Board (illustrative lenses)
- Positioning lens (competitive-alternatives method)
- Demand lens (owned-audience and lead engine)
- Retention lens (activation and habit)
- Metrics and focus lens (one North Star, ruthless prioritization)
- Trust and compliance lens (claims, data handling)

## Audit map
- Present: working product, first paying customers, a clear personal network.
- Thin: ICP is "compliance teams," too broad to target.
- Missing: roadmap, written backlog, a North Star metric.

## Selected findings
- **Convergence:** narrow the ICP to one regulated vertical first. Positioning, demand, and focus lenses all land here independently. Rank high.
- **Tension:** self-serve versus sales-led. The demand lens favors self-serve for two founders; the retention lens argues the first ten accounts need hand-holding to learn the job-to-be-done. Call: sales-led for the next [[N]] accounts, instrument for self-serve later.

## Prioritized backlog (scored, top three)
1. Pick one vertical and rewrite the site around it. Priority high (goal-fit 5, consensus 5, impact 4; effort 2, risk 2, reversibility-cost 1).
2. Stand up a weekly "regulatory digest" as a lead engine. Priority high (goal-fit 4, consensus 4, impact 4; effort 3, risk 1, reversibility-cost 1).
3. Define one North Star (weekly active accounts that read a summary). Priority medium-high.

## Framework left running
Council-weighted scoring, anchored to the MRR goal and the two-person constraint, written to `PRIORITIZATION-FRAMEWORK.md` so the founders' LLM keeps ranking new ideas the same way until they overrule it.

# Example: solo education creator

> Illustrative. Fictional composite. No real client data. Bracketed numbers are placeholders.

## Context
A solo creator teaches a hands-on skill to an audience of [[X]] followers and sells one paid course. Income is real but uneven. Content goes out often, revenue does not follow.

## Interview highlights
- Goal: replace [[$Y]] of other income with the creator business within a year.
- Constraint: solo, limited hours, will not become a daily-posting content machine.
- Definition of good: steady sales from work already made, not a treadmill.

## Board (illustrative lenses)
- Offer lens (value-stacked offers and pricing)
- Owned-audience lens (email over rented reach)
- Positioning lens (best-fit customer and category)
- Retention and membership lens (recurring revenue)
- Trust lens (honest claims, clear disclosures)

## Audit map
- Present: audience, one course, a real voice.
- Thin: the offer ladder stops at one product; email list is under-used.
- Missing: a North Star, a repeatable launch, a prioritized backlog.

## Selected findings
- **Convergence:** move the audience to email and build one repeatable launch. Owned-audience, offer, and retention lenses agree. Rank high.
- **Tension:** low-ticket volume versus a higher-ticket cohort. The offer lens likes a premium cohort for margin; the positioning lens warns it narrows the audience. Call: test a premium cohort to a small segment before committing.

## Prioritized backlog (scored, top three)
1. Add an email opt-in to every piece of content and move existing followers to the list. Priority high.
2. Package the existing course into one repeatable, dated launch. Priority high.
3. Test one premium cohort to a small segment. Priority medium.

## Framework left running
Council-weighted scoring anchored to the income-replacement goal and the "no treadmill" constraint, written to disk and overrulable.

# Example: local service business going full-time

> Illustrative. Fictional composite. No real client data. Bracketed numbers are placeholders.

## Context
A skilled local service provider runs a profitable side business and wants to make it the household's primary income. The work is good and clients are happy, but leads are inconsistent in a market the owner recently moved to.

## Interview highlights
- Goal: [[$Y]] per year from the business, enough to leave a separate job.
- Constraint: protected family time, a tight travel radius, one working weekend per month at most.
- Definition of good: a steady pipeline the owner does not have to worry about.

## Board (illustrative lenses)
- Local demand lens (referrals and local search)
- Pricing and margin lens (raise rates, sell higher-value packages)
- Positioning lens (a clear niche and best-fit client)
- Systems lens (outsource the dreaded work, protect time)
- Focus lens (highest-leverage move first)

## Audit map
- Present: strong craft, happy repeat clients, a working site.
- Thin: no referral engine, prices likely low for the target market.
- Missing: a written roadmap, a lead-tracking habit, a defined niche.

## Selected findings
- **Convergence:** the binding constraint is lead flow, not skill. Local demand, positioning, and focus lenses all point here. Rank first.
- **Tension:** volume now versus premium later. Pricing lens wants higher rates immediately; local demand lens wants reputation first. Call: build reputation and a referral engine now, raise prices on a defined schedule.

## Prioritized backlog (scored, top three)
1. Stand up a referral engine with existing happy clients and one local partner, and track every lead. Priority high.
2. Define one niche and rebuild the site's front page around it. Priority high.
3. Set a price-increase schedule tied to booking milestones. Priority medium.

## Framework left running
Council-weighted scoring, with the family-time limits set as hard filters (any option that breaks them is removed, not scored), written to disk and overrulable.
